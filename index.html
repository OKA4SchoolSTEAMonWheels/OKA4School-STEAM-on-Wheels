About

A portable model rooted in dignity, creativity, and access.

OKA4School STEAM on Wheels exists to reach learners where they are, especially in schools and communities with limited resources. Rather than waiting for young people to find opportunity, the program carries practical experiences to them through mobile circles that blend wellbeing, hands-on making, and future-focused learning.

As a CBO, the organization can begin locally, build trust at community level, and document a strong pilot model before expanding to wider partnerships and fundraising.

Program model

Three parts working together.

Wellbeing circles

Safe spaces for learners to talk, reflect, build peer support, and strengthen their sense of belonging in school.

Baking for agency

Practical sessions that build confidence, teamwork, creativity, and small income-generation skills through simple baking activities.

STEAM in everyday life

Low-cost, portable activities that connect science, technology, engineering, arts, and mathematics to things learners can see, touch, and create.

Objectives

What the organization is set up to do.

01

Learner wellbeing and retention

Create supportive school and community circles that help vulnerable learners stay engaged, seen, and supported.

02

Accessible STEAM learning

Deliver low-cost, portable activities that make STEAM feel relevant, creative, and achievable for underserved learners.

03

Local capacity and ownership

Work with teachers, youth leaders, and volunteers so the model can be adapted and sustained beyond one-off visits.

Pilot Route 1

A small, mobile pilot designed for proof of concept.

The first route can focus on one school or two nearby communities, using a portable kit and a short cycle of sessions to test what works best before scaling.

  • 8–10 circle sessions over one school term.
  • 30–40 learners reached through one pilot site.
  • Baking, reflection, and simple STEAM mini-activities in each cycle.
  • Light monitoring using attendance, reflections, and facilitator notes.
“We are building a model that can move lightly, meet learners close to their realities, and show them that learning can be both healing and transformative.”
