    About

    A portable model rooted in dignity, creativity, and access.

    Problem. •In Kenya, about 44% of adolescents (10–17) report a recent mental health problem and 12% meet criteria for a disorder(2022). Mental disorders are the second leading cause of disability for ages 10–24. Across many public and low-fee schools in Kenya, young people carry heavy emotional burdens into classrooms that are often overcrowded, exam-driven, and short on practical resources. Learners rarely have safe spaces to talk about how they feel, to make things with their hands, or to see how everyday activities like baking connect to science, creativity, and future opportunities. As a result, wellbeing needs go unnoticed, STEAM subjects feel intimidating or abstract, and many girls and quieter learners are left out of experiences that could build their confidence, curiosity, and sense of possibility.

    OKA4School STEAM on Wheels exists to reach learners where they are, especially in schools and communities with limited resources. Rather than waiting for young people to find opportunity, the program carries practical experiences to them through mobile circles that blend wellbeing, hands-on making, and future-focused learning.

    As a CBO, the organization began locally, built trust at the community level, and documented a pilot model with in-progress plans to expand to wider partnerships and fundraising.

    Our mission. To improve the well-being, confidence, and future‑readiness of young people in under‑resourced communities by turning everyday spaces into joyful, hands‑on learning circles that weave together psychosocial support, baking, and accessible STEAM experiences.

    Our vision. We envision a region where every learner—especially girls and those on the margins—has regular access to safe, creative spaces that nurture their wellbeing, spark their curiosity, and open real pathways into STEAM and sustainable livelihoods.

    Pilot Route 1

    A small, mobile pilot designed for proof of concept.

    The first route can focus on one school or two nearby communities, using a portable kit and a short cycle of sessions to test what works best before scaling.

    • 8–10 circle sessions over one school term.
    • 30–40 learners reached through one pilot site.
    • Baking, reflection, and simple STEAM mini-activities in each cycle.
    • Light monitoring using attendance, reflections, and facilitator notes.
